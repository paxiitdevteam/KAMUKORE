Littoral Region

Coastal culture with vibrant traditions and modern influences

Culture

Culture

The Littoral Region is home to vibrant coastal cultures, particularly the Duala and Bassa peoples. The region's history as a trading hub has created a unique blend of traditional and modern influences.

Traditional ceremonies, music, and dance remain central to community life, while the port city of Douala brings international perspectives and contemporary expressions of culture.

Craft

Craft

Traditional crafts include woodcarving, basket weaving, and textile production. Artisans create functional and decorative items that reflect coastal aesthetics and cultural symbols.

Modern craftspeople blend traditional techniques with contemporary designs, creating pieces that honor heritage while appealing to modern tastes.

The Littoral Region features coastal plains, mangrove forests, and river deltas. The Wouri River flows through the region, creating fertile areas for agriculture and fishing.

Mangrove ecosystems support diverse wildlife, while the coastline offers beautiful beaches and opportunities for water-based activities.
